Veteran US boxing writer John Condon called it "the greatest event I've ever worked on in my life." The bout was broadcast to 36 countries; promoters granted 760 press passes.

  • 1962 - Ali had a cameo role in the film version of Requiem for a Heavyweight, and during his exile from boxing, he starred in the short-lived 1969 Broadway musical, Buck White. He also appeared in the documentary film Black Rodeo riding both a horse and a bull.
  • 1963 -Ali released an album of spousic on Columbia Records titled, I Am the Greatest, and in 1964, he recorded a cover version of the rhythm and blues song "Stand by Me". I Am the Greatest sold 500,000 copies, and has been identified as an early example of rap music and a precursor to hip hop.
  • 2005 - President George W. Bush embraces Ali after presenting him with the Presidential Medal of Freedom, during ceremonies at the White House.
  • 2016 - dies June 3 at the age of 74 at Scottsdale, Arizona, U.S.
